Huawei Unveils Triple-Folding Smartphone

Huawei has introduced the world’s first triple-folding smartphone, priced at $2,800. The device unfolds into a tablet-sized screen and is designed for multitasking, gaming, and media consumption. It’s a glimpse into the future of mobile form factors

AI Designs Material to Cool Homes

Researchers have used AI to create a new material that cools buildings more efficiently than paint, potentially slashing energy bills. The tech could be applied to homes, clothing, and even vehicles, offering a smart solution to rising temperatures

Japan Launches World’s First Wooden Satellite

Japan has successfully launched LignoSat, a satellite made from magnolia wood, aiming to reduce space debris and burn cleanly upon reentry. Developed by Kyoto University and Sumitomo Forestry, it’s a bold step toward sustainable space tech

Breakthrough Battery Charges in Just 60 Seconds

A startup has unveiled a next-gen battery that can fully charge in under a minute using advanced graphene technology. Designed for smartphones and electric vehicles, it promises to revolutionize how we power up—fast, efficient, and eco-friendly.

AI Agents Are Becoming Game Dev Sidekicks

AI agents — not just assistants, but autonomous systems — are now helping developers write code, debug, and even design levels. OpenAI’s latest agent can browse the web, analyze data, and make decisions like a junior dev. Microsoft and Google are also rolling out tools that let AI collaborate with humans on game logic and storytelling. It’s not just about speeding up development — it’s about unlocking creativity by offloading the grunt work.

Google’s Gemini CLI Brings AI to the Terminal

For developers who live in the command line, Google’s new Gemini CLI tool lets you interact with AI directly from your terminal. You can ask it to generate code, debug, or even summarize documentation—without ever leaving your dev environment. It’s like having a coding buddy who speaks bash and Python fluently. This tool is part of Google’s push to make AI native to developer workflows, not just a fancy add-on

Splitgate 2 Returns to Beta After Player Feedback

The developers of Splitgate 2, a popular free-to-play shooter, are rolling the game back into beta after community feedback revealed issues with gameplay and stability. The original Splitgate is being shut down, and the studio is undergoing layoffs. It’s a reminder that player-driven development is still king in the gaming world
